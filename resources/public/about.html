About Epic Draft Club

What is Epic Draft Club?

Epic Draft Club is a web site for doing cube drafts of Epic Card Game. Use it to coordinate online drafts with your friends.

Click Here to Start a New Draft

Epic Draft Club is a no-frills drafting experience. It does one thing and one thing only: run Epic drafts. We pay our programmers nothing and pass those savings on to YOU. Here are the things that Epic Draft Club does NOT have:

  • Chat
  • Draft queues
  • Downloadable decks
  • Usernames and passwords
  • A virtual tabletop
  • A timer, goofgrabbing, click-and-drag, etc...

Instead, our users are encouraged to coordinate amongst themselves using existing tools like Discord and Cockatrice.

On the other hand, if you're a programmer who likes to do work for free, the source code for Epic Draft Club is on GitHub: http://github.com/wmorganjr/epic. It's written in Clojure and bad HTML and we welcome contributions.

How to Run a Draft

  1. Get a group of people together to play online.
  2. Go here: /new.html.
  3. Select the sets you want to draft with, and the number of cards in each pack. Reasonable defaults are provided for you.
  4. Enter the players' names in the text area provided.
  5. Click "Start Draft".
  6. What you get back is a list of links, one for each player. Send each player their link, which they'll follow to get to their seat. SAVE THESE LINKS. There is no way to get them back. If someone's Internet connection cuts out in the middle of the draft, someone else can open their link to make their picks for them.
  7. Take your seat and draft away!

How to Participate in a Draft

  1. Follow the link from the player who's running the draft, from Step 6, above.
  2. You'll be presented with your first pack. Select the card you want to take, and click the "Pick" button to the RIGHT of the card. Epic Draft Club takes no responsibility for mispicks because you clicked the wrong button.
  3. Wait for your next pack. The "Pick Summary" table at the top of the page will tell you how many cards each player has picked.
  4. If you want to look at the cards you've drafted already, click the "Drafted Cards" link. Click the "Pack" link to get back to your current pack.
  5. Your second and third packs will come to you immediately after you've drafted the final card in the previous pack. If you want to review your picks in between packs (e.g. to simulate the drafting at Worlds), wait until your pack contains one card, then review your picks, then take the last card and continue.
  6. Nothing special will happen after you take your last card. Click "Drafted Cards" to see the list of cards you drafted. If you want to build your deck and play with it, then you have to copy it manually into a program like Cockatrice.
